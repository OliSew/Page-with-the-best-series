Four Tankmen and a Dog (TV series)

The series tells the story of the crew of the 102 "Rudy" tank, which is going through the combat route of the Armored Brigade. Heroes of Westerplatte from Studzianki to Berlin. The crew consists of: a teenage Gdańsk citizen, Janek Kos, whom the war threw to distant Siberia, a Silesian Gustlik Jeleń, a refugee from the German army, a Russian tank commander, a descendant of Polish exiles Olgierd Jarosz and Georgian Grigory Saakashvili, Janek's friend from Siberia; The crew is supplemented by Szarik, Janek's dog, who more than once saves the crew from oppression. After Olgierd's death, Janek becomes the commander, and the crew is joined by the son of a peasant from Studzianki - Tomek Czereśniak. The brigade is commanded by a Colonel, who looks after the tank crew in a fatherly way. In the Battle of Studzianki, a local peasant, Czereśniak, joins the crew as a guide. Then "Rudy", christened after the color of Marusia's hair, arrives near Warsaw, where he is destroyed and the crew is taken to the hospital. After convalescence, the armored personnel reach their tank near Kołobrzeg. Unfortunately, it turns out that Lieutenant Jarosz is dead and the tank was badly damaged again. Janek becomes the new commander, and the Polish partisan "West" is temporarily assigned to the crew. After reaching the Baltic Sea, it turns out that he is Janek's missing father.
